The Irishman, but from Queens

April 24, 2022 by Alvi

In the modern Millenial age, we over-value the act of being busy all the time. We don't call our parents, our partners, or our friends. In such a world, we need more people like the gentleman I met at Starbucks today.

After ordering my overpriced baconeggandcheese sandwhich from a Starbucks, I turned around to find a seat where I could scarf down my sandwich. I had no intention of enjoying the sandwhich. It would just be nice to eat the sandwhich away from the prying eyes of my fellow New Yorkers who would judge me for supporting an establishment that represents the anti-thesis of our city.

I spot a table in the corner of the room by a large floor-to-ceiling window. Although the table is not occupied, there is a elderly man leaned up against a wall that is adjacent to the table. I approach him, and in the universal language of New York City, I say:

"You good?"

Getting a better look at him now, I can see his pale & unflushed skin under his double-layered N-95 masks. He wore a cotton cardigan over a plaid grey button down, thick grey sweatpants, and a pair of worn in New Balances. His greyed hair was thinning, but he made sure to maintain its dignity, sweeping it delicately to his right side. He must have been dressing like this for years now.

He replies, "Yeah of course! I don't know why everyone always asks me!"

Probably cause you're hovering pretty close to the table there, gramps. I thought to myself.

I chuckled, threw a thumbs up, and sat down to begin unraveling my overpriced bread. Usually, this is the extent to which New Yorkers will communicate with one another. And to be clear, we are very content with that reality. If you took a nanoscope to examine our DNA, you'd see we mostly mind our own damn business (and you should too, punk.)

But to my surprise, the gentleman breaks the silence that prevalied to ask me: "What kinda mask is that?"

"Oh this...?" I gesture to my cheap, plain, black mask sitting on the table."This is just something to keep my alive."

There's a pause as he inspects my mask further. He reaches into his worn-out, leather fanny pack and begins digging for something. After zipping about for a moment, he pulls out a brand-new, plastic-sheathed, white KN95 mask.

Without missing a beat, he slides it across the table to me.

"Use this; they sell these suckers for so much now online...but I get 'em for free from the CVS!"
